How It Works

A beginner-friendly explanation of graph-based friend recommendations.

1Build the Social Graph

All users and their friendships are stored as an undirected graph using the NetworkX library.

  • Each node represents one user.
  • Each edge (line) between two nodes means they are already friends.
  • The friendship is undirected: if Alice knows Bob, Bob knows Alice.
2Find Candidates

For the selected user, the algorithm looks at every other user who is not yet a direct friend. These are the "candidates" for a new connection.

3Count Mutual Friends

For each candidate, the algorithm counts how many users are friends with both the selected user and the candidate. These shared connections are called mutual friends.

mutuals = friends_of(Alice) ∩ friends_of(David)

∩ means intersection — users who appear in both sets.

4Rank & Display

Candidates are sorted by their mutual-friend count — highest first. Only users with at least one mutual friend appear in the recommendations list. The top 5 are shown.

Example

Suppose Alice is friends with Bob and Carol.
David is also friends with Bob and Carol — but not Alice.
David has 2 mutual friends with Alice, so he ranks at the top of her recommendation list.
